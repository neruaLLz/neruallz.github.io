To be honest, I have no idea what to write every day. Should I keep a diary in English, or should I just write about what I’ve learned today? For example, I finally figured out the differences between engineer, constructor, general contractor, and construction contractor.

An engineer is responsible for the technical aspects of a project, including designing, planning, and even ensuring compliance with regulations (which, to my surprise, goes beyond what I initially expected). The term constructor is less commonly used in the United States but is more popular in European countries.

In my opinion, general contractor is the most commonly used term in the construction industry. A general contractor focuses on overseeing the entire project and managing contractors or subcontractors. It can refer to either a company or an individual responsible for the whole project. On the other hand, a construction contractor is quite similar to a general contractor but places more emphasis on assigning tasks to subcontractors—or at least, that’s how I understand it.
