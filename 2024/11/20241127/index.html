an improved version by my dearest AI teacher:

Okay. Look no further. Let’s dive into what I’ve learned so far from the book Everybody Writes. One of the key principles of good writing is clarity, brevity, and utility. This trio truly resonates with me—it’s like a writer’s north star. But here’s where I hit a roadblock: I wasn’t sure what to write next.

A few days ago, I came across an article on The Verge. It was a detailed and honest review of our competitors, striking a balance between positive and negative points. An aside: the competitor boldly claims to be the “first of its kind,” but let’s call a spade a spade—that’s not true. Still, their presence feels significant; they’re truly breathing down our neck.

As for us, with all that reputation comes serious responsibility—and a need for deep expertise. It’s a reminder that staying ahead in this game requires more than just innovation; it demands excellence, too. (What I just said…╥﹏╥)
